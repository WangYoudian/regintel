# FCA Policy Statement PS21/3 — Operational Resilience

**Source:** Financial Conduct Authority  
**Published:** March 2021  
**Effective:** March 2022 (phased implementation through March 2025)

---

## 1. Scope and Application

1.1 This Policy Statement sets out final rules on operational resilience for FCA-authorised firms, including banks, building societies, investment firms, and payment institutions.

1.2 Firms must identify their important business services and set impact tolerances for each service.

1.3 The rules apply to all firms within the scope of the Senior Managers and Certification Regime (SM&CR).

## 2. Identification of Important Business Services

2.1 Firms must identify and document all important business services they provide. An important business service is one whose disruption could cause intolerable harm to consumers or threaten market integrity.

2.2 For each important business service, firms must set an impact tolerance for the maximum acceptable level of disruption. Impact tolerances must be reviewed and approved by the governing body at least annually.

2.3 Firms must maintain a mapping of important business services to the people, processes, technology, facilities, and third-party dependencies that underpin them. This mapping must be kept up to date and reviewed at least quarterly.

## 3. Testing and Exercising

3.1 Firms must test their ability to remain within impact tolerances across a range of severe but plausible scenarios. Testing must include:
   - (a) Cyber-attack scenarios, including ransomware and supply chain compromise
   - (b) Technology and infrastructure failures
   - (c) Third-party service provider disruptions
   - (d) Physical security incidents affecting key locations
   - (e) Extreme but plausible market events

3.2 Testing of operational resilience must be conducted at least annually. Firms must document test scenarios, results, and remediation plans.

3.3 Firms must participate in sector-wide resilience testing as directed by the FCA.

## 4. Communication and Reporting

4.1 Firms must notify the FCA immediately (and no later than 24 hours after becoming aware) if an operational disruption could breach or has breached an impact tolerance for an important business service.

4.2 Firms must submit an annual operational resilience report to the FCA. The report must include:
   - (a) A summary of important business services and their impact tolerances
   - (b) Results of resilience testing conducted during the reporting period
   - (c) Any breaches of impact tolerances and remediation actions taken
   - (d) Changes to the mapping of important business services
   - (e) Governance body's assessment of overall operational resilience

4.3 The annual report must be approved by the firm's governing body before submission.

## 5. Governance and Oversight

5.1 The governing body is ultimately responsible for operational resilience. This responsibility cannot be delegated.

5.2 Firms must appoint a senior manager as the Operational Resilience lead. This individual is responsible for the day-to-day implementation of the resilience framework.

5.3 The Operational Resilience lead must provide a quarterly update to the governing body on:
   - (a) Current resilience status against impact tolerances
   - (b) Results of any testing conducted
   - (c) Actual or near-breaches of impact tolerances
   - (d) Progress against remediation plans

5.4 Operational resilience policies and procedures must be reviewed at least annually.

## 6. Third-Party Management

6.1 Firms must assess the operational resilience of all critical third-party service providers. This assessment must be conducted at least annually.

6.2 Contracts with critical third parties must include:
   - (a) Clearly defined service levels aligned with the firm's impact tolerances
   - (b) Rights for the firm to audit the third party's resilience arrangements
   - (c) Obligations for the third party to report operational disruptions that could affect the firm
   - (d) Exit provisions that ensure continuity of service in the event of contract termination

6.3 Firms must maintain an up-to-date register of all third-party arrangements that support important business services.

## 7. Self-Assessment and Documentation

7.1 Firms must prepare and maintain a comprehensive Operational Resilience Document that includes:
   - (a) Description of important business services
   - (b) Impact tolerances for each service
   - (c) Mapping of dependencies
   - (d) Testing schedule and results
   - (e) Remediation plans
   - (f) Governance structure for resilience

7.2 The Operational Resilience Document must be made available to the FCA upon request.

7.3 Firms must be able to demonstrate to the FCA, on a continuing basis, that they are meeting the requirements set out in this Policy Statement.

## 8. Transitional Arrangements

8.1 Firms must have fully implemented the requirements by 31 March 2025.

8.2 By 31 March 2022, firms must have identified their important business services and set impact tolerances.

8.3 By 31 March 2023, firms must have completed the first mapping of dependencies for each important business service.

8.4 By 31 March 2024, firms must have completed the first full cycle of resilience testing.

## 9. Record-Keeping

9.1 Firms must retain all documentation related to operational resilience for a minimum of six years.

9.2 Records must include:
   - (a) Risk assessments and impact tolerance documentation
   - (b) Test plans, results, and remediation actions
   - (c) Third-party resilience assessments
   - (d) Governing body meeting minutes discussing operational resilience
   - (e) Operational disruption incident reports
